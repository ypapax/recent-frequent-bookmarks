# Privacy Policy for Recently Used Bookmarks & History

**Last Updated: 2025-11-05**

## Overview
Recently Used Bookmarks & History is a Chrome extension that helps you quickly access your most frequently and recently used bookmarks and browsing history.

## Data Collection and Usage

### What Data We Access
This extension requires access to:
- **Bookmarks**: To display your bookmarked pages
- **Browsing History**: To show visit counts, last visit times, and usage patterns
- **Local Storage**: To save your preferences (hidden items, filter states, search queries)
- **Tabs**: To open bookmarks in new tabs when clicked

### How We Use Your Data
All data processing happens **locally on your device**:
- Your bookmarks and history are read from your browser to display in the extension
- Visit patterns are analyzed locally to calculate scores and highlight time-of-day matches
- Your preferences (hidden items, filters) are stored in Chrome's local storage

### What We DON'T Do
- ❌ We do NOT send any data to external servers
- ❌ We do NOT track your browsing behavior
- ❌ We do NOT collect personal information
- ❌ We do NOT share your data with third parties
- ❌ We do NOT use analytics or tracking services

## Data Storage
- All preferences are stored locally using Chrome's `chrome.storage.local` API
- No data is transmitted over the network
- No data is stored on external servers
- Uninstalling the extension removes all stored preferences

## Permissions Explained
- **bookmarks**: Required to read and display your bookmarks
- **history**: Required to show visit counts and times
- **storage**: Required to save your settings and hidden items
- **tabs**: Required to open bookmarks when clicked

## Changes to Privacy Policy
We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted in the extension's store listing and README file.

## Contact
If you have questions about this privacy policy, please [create an issue on our GitHub repository](https://github.com/ypapax/recently_used_bookmark_policy/issues).

## Your Rights
You can:
- View all data the extension accesses (it's all your local browser data)
- Delete stored preferences by using the "Unignore All" feature in Settings
- Remove the extension at any time to delete all stored preferences
